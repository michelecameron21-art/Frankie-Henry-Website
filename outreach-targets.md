## 1. CHILDREN'S BOOK REVIEW BLOGS (3 Targets)

---

### Target 1A: Kid's Picture Book Review

- **Type:** Children's picture book review blog and influencer
- **URL:** https://www.kidspicturebookreview.com/
- **Contact:** matt@kidspicturebookreview.com
- **Submission process:** Send a short email with book title, genre, page length, word count, target age range, and what makes your book special. Do NOT send attachments unless asked. Have a clean digital copy ready (no watermarks).
- **Fee:** Free
- **Why they're a good fit:** They specifically champion self-published children's picture books. They review books and post across Instagram, Goodreads, Google, and Amazon. They also offer author interviews and have a "kids review books" feature where actual children review your book -- perfect for the 4-8 age range.

**Ready-to-send message:**

> Subject: Review Request -- Frankie & Henry and the Brave River Rescue
>

It's a story about courage, brotherly love, and helping others -- with a cast of wild animal friends and vibrant safari illustrations that little ones adore.
>
> I'd be delighted to send you a complimentary digital or physical copy, whichever you prefer. I think your young reviewers would have brilliant fun with this one!
>
> You can find the book here: https://www.amazon.com/dp/B0GTVVPPH6
> And our website: https://frankiehenryadventures.com
>
> Thank you so much for your time. I truly appreciate what you do for indie authors like me.
>
> Warm regards,
> Michele Cameron

### Target 1C: The Children's Book Review (TCBR)

- **Type:** Established children's book review site
- **URL:** https://www.thechildrensbookreview.com/
- **Submission page:** https://www.thechildrensbookreview.com/media-kit/submission-guidelines/
- **Contact:** Submit via online form on their website
- **Fee:** Editorial Review: $5 (digital) or $20 (physical). Dedicated Review: $289 (standard, 4-5 weeks) or $389 (accelerated, 1-2 weeks). The $5 Editorial Review is the best starting point -- no guarantee of publication but very affordable.
- **Why they're a good fit:** TCBR is one of the most established children's book review platforms. They explicitly accept both traditionally and self-published books. A review here carries weight and credibility. Even the affordable Editorial Review option gets your book in front of their editorial team.

- **Fee:** Free (they don't pay contributors, but you get a byline, author bio, and social media promotion)

A guest post about reading with your children, the importance of adventure stories for ages 4-8, or how pets inspire creativity in kids would fit perfectly. You'd get a backlink to your Amazon listing and website, plus exposure to their parent audience.

## SUGGESTED OUTREACH ORDER

1. **Start with the free review blogs** (Kid's Picture Book Review and Independent Book Review) -- these take time, so get them submitted first.
2. **Join the Facebook groups** and start engaging naturally before posting about your book (give it a few days of genuine participation first).
3. **DM the Bookstagram accounts** -- keep it warm and personal, no pressure.
4. **Pitch the podcast** -- this is a longer lead time but high value.
5. **Submit the guest post pitch** to AmyandRose.
6. **Consider TCBR's $5 editorial review** once the free options are underway.

Good luck, Michele!
